Integrated Signal: What the Data May Suggest for LVMH China
⚠ These are observational signals, not investment recommendations. Correlations are indicative and should be verified with additional sources before drawing conclusions.
Retail Sales: Deceleration then partial recovery
⚠ Mixed — monitor next releases
Retail growth decelerated from +6.4% (May-25) to +0.9% (Dec-25), then partially recovered to +2.8% (Jan-Feb 26). It is possible this signals stabilization, but one data point is insufficient to confirm a trend reversal. NBS notes growth rates are not adjusted for price factors.
Consumer Confidence: Persistently below neutral
🔴 Structural headwind for discretionary spending
Confidence has remained below 91 pts (vs. neutral 100) since mid-2022. This persistently depressed level is consistent with cautious consumer behavior. Luxury spending, being highly discretionary, is typically among the first categories affected when confidence weakens.
Gold & Jewelry: Volatile and price-distorted
⚠ Revenue growth ≠ demand recovery — interpret with caution
The series ranges from +6.1% (Jun-25) to +37.6% (Oct-25) in a single year, reflecting gold price volatility rather than stable consumer demand shifts. NBS explicitly notes these figures are not adjusted for price factors, making them a noisy proxy for actual jewelry demand.

📊 Data Sources & Methodology
①
WeChat Index: Weighted engagement score from 1.38B monthly active users (~96% of China adults). Measures public content volume + engagement. Primary signal for mainland consumer sentiment.
②
Google Trends: Relative popularity 0–100. China data = VPN users (~3%) — tech-savvy, internationally-oriented. Use as directional confirmation only. Global data fully representative.
③
NBS Data: Official China government statistics. Monthly retail YoY% — NOT adjusted for price factors. Jan & Feb combined (Lunar New Year). Published ~15th at stats.gov.cn/english.
④
Resale (Foxytotes / Watchcharts): VR = resale price ÷ retail. Weighted avg by MSRP/retail per brand. US/Western-centric — does not capture CN (Plum, Xianyu), JP or ME resale. 🟡 = top-5 fallback method. Values are directional indicators, not absolute benchmarks.
⑤
Disclaimer: Research tool only — not investment advice. Each source measures a different dimension of consumer behavior. Always combine with LVMH/peer earnings and professional financial analysis before drawing conclusions.
